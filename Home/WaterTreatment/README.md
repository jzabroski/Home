## Spin-down Filter
**A high-quality reusable spin-down sediment filter (typically 40–100 micron rating) is the ideal pre-filter.** It protects your 10-year carbon + KDF backwash media tank by removing larger particulates like sand, silt, rust, dirt, and debris from city water lines or pipe scale. This prevents premature clogging or channeling in the carbon/KDF bed, extending its effective lifespan for PFAS and chloramine reduction.

### Why This Setup Works for Your Goals
- **Sediment protection only**: Spin-down filters handle mechanical filtration (larger particles). They do not meaningfully affect dissolved contaminants like **PFAS** or **chloramines**—that's the job of your downstream carbon + KDF media.
- **Carbon + KDF effectiveness**: Granular activated carbon (especially catalytic carbon) + KDF can reduce chloramines (KDF helps with chlorine/chloramine and some metals; carbon handles the rest) and a good portion of PFAS (particularly longer-chain ones in PFAS6 like PFOA/PFOS). Performance depends on contact time, media volume/quality, and flow rate. Pre-filtering sediment keeps the media cleaner for better adsorption.
- City water often has low-to-moderate sediment, so a spin-down is low-maintenance and cost-effective compared to disposable cartridge pre-filters.

### Recommended Spin-Down Filters
Popular, well-reviewed options for whole-house pre-filtration before carbon systems:

- **Rusco Spin-Down / Sediment Trapper** (highly recommended in many expert setups): Available in 60-mesh (~100–250 micron) or finer screens. Reusable stainless steel or polyester screen; flushes easily. Excellent for city or well water as a first-stage protector. The "Trapper" version holds more sediment.
- **iSpring WSP series** (e.g., WSP-50): 50-micron, brass or composite housing, flushable/reusable. Good flow rates (up to 20+ GPM) and popular for municipal water.
- Other solid alternatives: Similar models from Aquaboon, Waterdrop, or SpringWell's own spin-down (often ~90 micron).

**Micron rating guidance for city water**:
- **50–100 micron** is the sweet spot as a pre-filter. It catches visible/heavier sediment without restricting flow much or needing frequent flushing.
- Start with ~50–60 micron (or 100 mesh) if you have noticeable rust/scale. Coarser (e.g., 100+ micron) if sediment is very light.
- Finer than ~30–40 micron can clog faster in a spin-down and may not be necessary upstream of your main tank.

**Installation tips**:
- Place it **right after the main water shutoff/meter** (before the carbon+KDF tank).
- Use full-port ball valves and unions for easy servicing.
- Flush the spin-down periodically (every few months or when pressure drops) by opening the bottom valve—very little water waste.
- Match pipe size (¾" or 1" common) and ensure it handles your peak flow rate (e.g., 10–20+ GPM for whole house).

### Additional Advice for PFAS6 + Chloramines
Your carbon + KDF backwash filter should handle the bulk of the work, but results vary with specific PFAS types (longer-chain ones are captured better), water chemistry, and media contact time. For maximum PFAS reduction, some users add a point-of-use reverse osmosis (RO) or specialized PFAS cartridge under the kitchen sink.

Test your water before/after installation (look for labs offering PFAS6 testing) to verify performance. If your city water has high chloramines, confirm your main filter uses **catalytic carbon** (superior to standard GAC for chloramines).

This combo (spin-down pre-filter → carbon+KDF backwash) is a standard, effective approach for city water treatment. Brands like Rusco or iSpring are reliable starting points—check current specs/reviews for your pipe size and flow needs.
